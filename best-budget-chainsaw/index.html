Best budget chainsaws

Quick answer

The Oregon CS1500 is our top pick for the best budget chainsaw – it offers reliable gas-powered performance at a price that won‘t break the bank.

The Stihl MS 251 is the best premium option for heavy-duty users, while the Echo CS-310 is the best compact choice for occasional light work or tight spaces.

A budget chainsaw doesn’t have to mean weak performance or poor durability. Whether you’re clearing storm debris, pruning trees, or cutting firewood, the right affordable gas or electric model can handle the job without the high cost of professional-grade gear. When choosing, focus on bar length (most homeowners do fine with a 16- or 18-inch bar), engine displacement for gas saws or amp-hours for electric, and safety features like a chain brake and low-kickback bar. Keep in mind that budget saws often compromise on vibration dampening, air filtration, and long-term parts availability. That doesn’t make them bad – it just means you should match the saw to your expected workload. For occasional use, a well-reviewed budget chainsaw can last years with proper maintenance. For frequent heavy cutting, it’s worth stepping up to a premium model that handles sustained use better.

Best for homeowner light use

If your chainsaw use is limited to clearing storm branches, cutting a few trees for firewood, or occasional property maintenance, the Oregon CS1500 offers the best balance of affordability and reliable gas performance. Best for heavy-duty professional use

For users who cut every day or need to handle large timber, the Stihl MS 251 is built to endure. Its engine delivers strong torque, and the dealer network makes maintenance straightforward. While it costs significantly more, the durability, lower vibration, and parts support mean it will outlast multiple budget saws for those who demand a workhorse. Our pick: Stihl MS 251.

Best for small properties and tight spaces

When you need a saw that’s easy to carry up a ladder or maneuver around crowded branches, the Echo CS-310 shines. It’s light enough to use with one hand for precision cuts, starts quickly, and vibrates far less than most compact gas saws. This is the best option for homeowners with small lots, lots of pruning, or anyone who prioritizes comfort over pure cutting power. Our pick: Echo CS-310.

Our verdict

Choosing the best budget chainsaw really comes down to how often you cut and what you’re cutting. For most homeowners, the Oregon CS1500 delivers the best value – strong performance at a low price. If you have more demanding needs or want a saw that will last through daily use, the Stihl MS 251 is worth the premium. And if space and weight are your main concerns, the compact Echo CS-310 is a nimble, reliable companion. No matter which you pick, always pair your saw with proper safety gear and keep it well maintained.
