Best budget chainsaws

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

For most people looking for a dependable saw under a few hundred dollars, the Husqvarna 120 Mark II is the clear top pick, blending proven Swedish engineering with approachable running costs.

The Stihl MS 261 C-M suits pros who need professional-grade power and lower emissions, while the Black+Decker LCS1020 is ideal for light-duty work where cordless convenience matters most.

A good budget chainsaw isn’t about cutting the most corners, it’s about getting reliable, safe cutting power without spending more than necessary. Whether you’re pruning overgrown branches, cutting firewood for the weekend, or tackling occasional storm cleanup, a capable saw in the affordable range can handle the job if you pick the right one. When choosing, focus on engine displacement or battery voltage, bar length that matches your typical work (most tasks need 14 to 18 inches), and ease of maintenance like tool-free chain tensioning. Budget models often trade a bit of refinement or long-term durability for a lower price, but the best ones still deliver solid starts, decent anti-vibration, and a chain that stays sharp. Avoid the very cheapest no-name saws, they’re often impossible to repair and dangerous.

Best for specific needs

Best for general yard work and firewood

If you’re maintaining a typical suburban property or cutting a few cords of firewood each season, the Husqvarna 120 Mark II hits the sweet spot between price and capability. Its moderate power handles most tasks up to 16-inch logs, and the low vibration and easy start features make it comfortable for afternoon sessions. It’s not a professional saw, but it’s far more reliable than other budget options. Our pick: Husqvarna 120 Mark II.

Best for all-day professional use

When your livelihood depends on a saw that starts every time and delivers consistent power through a full tank, the Stihl MS 261 C-M is the clear investment. M-Tronic eliminates guesswork, and the durability stands up to daily abuse on tree crews or firewood operations. The higher upfront cost pays off in fewer breakdowns and better fuel efficiency over years of heavy use. Our pick: Stihl MS 261 C-M.

Best for lightweight cordless convenience

For homeowners who mainly trim branches and want to avoid the hassle of mixing fuel or dealing with noise complaints, the Black+Decker LCS1020 offers a refreshingly simple alternative. Its light weight and instant start make it a joy for quick jobs, and it stores easily without worrying about stale fuel. Just keep an extra battery on hand if you have more than an hour of cutting planned. Our pick: Black+Decker LCS1020.

Our verdict

Ultimately, the best budget chainsaw for you depends on how often you cut and what size material you tackle. The Husqvarna 120 Mark II offers the most balanced package for the vast majority of homeowners, providing years of service for a modest investment. If your work is lighter and your yard is small, the Black+Decker LCS1020 is a near-perfect cordless companion. And if you’re in the field every day, the Stihl MS 261 C-M will earn back its premium price many times over. Stick with a saw from a brand with dealer support nearby, and you’ll avoid the frustration of a dead saw mid-job.
