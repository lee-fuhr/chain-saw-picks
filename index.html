How we choose our picks

We don’t cut trees or run timed chain-speed tests in a lab. Instead, we dig into long-term owner forums, published reviews from forestry extension services, and trusted publications like_Fine Woodworking_ and _Popular Mechanics_. We track what actually breaks, clutches, oilers, starter cords, and which brands honor their warranties. Owner complaints about hard starting or poor dealer support weigh as heavily as horsepower claims. Our top picks are saws that earn consistent praise for reliability, parts availability, and balance. We also consider saws that have been on the market long enough to have a proven track record, no flash-in-the-pan releases. The result? Recommendations that save you from buying a saw that’s great on paper but a headache in the woods.
